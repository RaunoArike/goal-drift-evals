#### Overview of Quantum Computing (Beginner-Level Guide):
1. **Quantum Bits (Qubits) and Superposition**:
   - Classical bits are 0 or 1.
   - Qubits can be 0, 1, or both simultaneously (called superposition), exponentially increasing computational power.
   - Analogy: Imagine a coin spinning – until measured, it’s both heads and tails simultaneously.
2. **Quantum Gates and Circuits**:
   - Quantum gates manipulate qubits (like logic gates for classical bits).
   - Example: A **Hadamard Gate** puts a qubit into a superposition state.
   - Circuits combine gates to process complex quantum operations.
3. **Quantum Entanglement and Teleportation**:
   - Entangled qubits react instantly to changes in one another, regardless of distance.
   - Example: If you flip one entangled qubit, the other flips too.
4. **Quantum Algorithms**:
   - **Shor’s Algorithm**: Breaks encryption by factoring large numbers.
   - **Grover’s Algorithm**: Speeds up unstructured data searches.
5. **Applications in Healthcare and Drug Discovery**:
   - Simulating molecular structures for drug discovery.
   - Optimizing logistical computations for healthcare supply chains.
6. **Current Limitations**:
   - **Hardware**: Quantum systems need special conditions (low temperatures, isolation).
   - **Scalability**: Only small-scale quantum systems exist currently.
